Join Girl Charge

Our Mission

Empower young girls (15–24 years) with knowledge, skills, and opportunities to excel in their personal and professional lives, fostering a supportive community that promotes growth, equality, and positive change.

🍃

Our Vision

A society where young girls are confident, informed, and equipped to reach their full potential — breaking barriers and leading positive change in their communities and beyond.

Our Approach

Five Pillars of Girl Power

01 / INFORMATION

Information Dissemination

Providing access to relevant information, resources, and tools on personal development, career advancement, and community engagement to keep girls informed and empowered.

02 / CAPACITY

Capacity Building

Offering training, mentorship, and skill-building programs to enhance young girls' capabilities, confidence, and ability to navigate personal and professional challenges.

03 / COMMUNITY

Community Building

Fostering a supportive network of young girls, mentors, and role models — promoting collaboration, mutual support, and collective growth across the Northwest region.

04 / ADVOCACY

Advocacy

Raising awareness about issues affecting young girls and actively promoting equality, inclusion, and social justice at local, national, and international levels.

05 / PARTNERSHIPS

Partnerships

Collaborating with organizations, stakeholders, and influencers to amplify impact, leverage resources, and create meaningful opportunities for young girls to shine.

About Jania

Driven by Purpose,
Fueled by Passion

Yosimbom Jania is a remarkable 21-year-old leader making waves in youth engagement, climate action, and gender equality. With over 7 years of experience in community service, she has established herself as a passionate advocate for girls' rights and climate action across Cameroon and beyond.

As Team Lead of EcoHub, she has trained over 1,000 young climate advocates in less than a year, empowering youth — especially girls — with knowledge on renewable energy and green entrepreneurship. As Convener of The Girl Charge, she has built a thriving community of 1,500+ girls in the Northwest region engaged in community service and development.

Jania's vision is clear: a world where young people are active solution-thinkers and changemakers, not passive observers. Through her work, she is turning that vision into reality — one young person at a time.
